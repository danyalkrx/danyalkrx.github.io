The Effect of Changing pH and Partial Pressure on Hemoglobin-Oxygen Affinity in Ovis aries Blood using Oxygen Dissociation Curves

04-2022

A lab report which analyzed the effect of changing pH and pO2 on oxygen saturation in Ovis aries blood. The two pH values were 6.8 and 7.2, being slightly acidic and basic respectively. The experiment found that a lower pH causes the initial hemoglobin saturation to be lower and the difference in P50 demonstrate the Bohr effect. The study concluded that the Bohr Effect is beneficial to the body because it affects the rate at which oxygen dissociates from hemoglobin, allowing for higher uptake during exercise. Data was calculated and presented in graphs using R.
