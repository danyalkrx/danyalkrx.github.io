Ghorbani Lab
Neuroimmunology and Multiple Sclerosis Research

Our research program explores why the brain’s capacity for myelin repair declines with age. This loss of regenerative ability plays a significant role in the transition from relapsing to progressive MS. My lab is particularly interested in the contributions of telomere shortening, cellular senescence and changes in the extracellular matrix (ECM) to impaired remyelination during aging. Our aim is to identify therapeutic targets that rejuvenate the aging central nervous system and restore its regenerative capacity, thereby enhancing remyelination and slowing MS progression. Beyond basic research, I am committed to training the next generation of scientists through inclusive mentorship and collaborative learning.
